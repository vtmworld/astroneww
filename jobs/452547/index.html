About the role:

As a Lead Product Researcher within our Inspire & Motivate team, youll be at the forefront of Targets evolving digital experience, shaping it for our guests and contributing to business growth. You will evolve how our guests discover newness and inspiration across Targets end to end experience, with a deep focus on our Homepage and Category experiences. Your work will come to life on Targets digital touchpoints including web, mobile web, and native applications. You will play a crucial role in driving the product development process, ensuring that our experiences align with user needs, business objectives, and technological feasibility.

As a Lead Product Researcher you will certify relevance and quality of insights through thoughtful assessment of risk, establishment of clear success criteria, and the use of methodology based on sound research principles and practices. In addition, you will serve as a catalyst to educate, accelerate, and activate product design and development to be masters of guest needs and apply that into their product.

Responsibilities:

Serve as a leader throughout the research project life cycle  recommending appropriate methodology, scoping, and coordinating the entire process, facilitating in-field research and post-research synthesis through analysis

Provide expert guidance in leading, conducting, and synthesizing qualitative research projects  including, but not limited to: in person moderated testing, remote moderated & unmoderated testing, surveys, heuristic reviews, benchmark studies, concept testing, online & in-store intercepts, ethnographies, shop-alongs

Lead synthesis and analysis of research findings  activating the insights gleaned through observations and interviews and identifying key opportunity areas in the context of corporate strategic direction

Use your influence to identify and pursue user-centric outcomes within and across products

Effectively facilitate multiple cross functional working teams and collaborate across multiple design and product teams

Guide research process and mentor junior researchers

Advance a culture of human-centered practice within UX, product, and engineering teams

Build a network of relationships to enable your vision in service of our guest experience and Target as a whole

About you:

7+ years experience as a product researcher with a strong portfolio showcasing successful case studies

Preferred 4-year degree in Anthropology, Social Science, Design Research, Psychology, Human Factors, HCI, or related field

Genuine curiosity about people and experiences

Excellent written and verbal communication skills; demonstrated ability to effectively communicate complex concepts to cross functional partners

A strong ability to tell compelling stories from data

Comfortable with ambiguity and can easily adjust to changing priorities

Strong project management skills  experience managing and prioritizing multiple projects at various stages in the project life cycle

Advanced strategic problem-solving, systems thinking, and collaboration skills

You balance the need for near-term solutions and long-term vision

You are an expert in communicating how research connects to, serves, and improves business outcomes

Exhibit a growth mindset

Comfortable speaking to a diverse group of people and leading conversations to uncover research insights

Experience running, recruiting, moderating and analyzing research and thinking strategically about their application
