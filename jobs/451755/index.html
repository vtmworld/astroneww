Position Summary

The OhioRISE Population Health Specialist primary responsibility role will be to support Population level improvements through the develop, implement, and manage value-added benefits programs that go above and beyond the standard Medicaid benefits package, specifically designed to meet the unique needs of youth with complex behavioral health conditions.

Responsibilities:  Design and develop value-added benefits programs tailored to the specific needs of youth with behavioral health conditions within the OhioRISE managed care plan.  Collaborate with internal teams, including clinical staff, behavioral health specialists, and care coordinators, to identify gaps in existing benefits and develop innovative solutions to address these gaps.  Conduct research and analysis to identify evidence-based practices, interventions, and services that can be incorporated into the value-added benefits programs.  Work closely with network providers, community organizations, and external stakeholders to establish partnerships and negotiate contracts for the delivery of value-added services.  Monitor and evaluate the effectiveness of value-added benefits programs through data analysis, member feedback, and outcomes measurement to ensure continuous improvement and alignment with member needs.  Stay up-to-date with the latest trends, research, and best practices in the field of behavioral health to inform the development and enhancement of value-added benefits.  Collaborate with the Communication Director and outreach teams to effectively communicate the availability and benefits of value-added programs to members, providers, and other stakeholders.  Ensure compliance with all relevant regulatory requirements and guidelines related to the OhioRISE Provider agreement.  Collaborate with the other Ohio Medicaid Managed Care Planes to support and inform value-added benefits strategies.  Collaborate with the OhioRISE Flex Fund Administrator to identify Flex Fund utilization trends and determine if a VAB is available that can meet the identified need.  Provide training and support to internal staff and network providers on the value-added benefits programs and how to effectively utilize and promote them.

Required Qualifications  Bachelor's degree in healthcare administration, public health, social work, or a related field.  2 years of experience in healthcare administration, managed care, or a related field. A focus on behavioral health services for youth, preferred  Excellent analytical and problem-solving skills, with the ability to gather and interpret data to inform decision-making  Proficiency in using data analysis tools and software applications  1 year of project management experience with the ability to prioritize tasks, manage multiple projects simultaneously, and meet deadlines

Preferred Qualifications  Strong knowledge and understanding of Medicaid managed care programs, regulations, and compliance requirements.  Experience in developing and managing value-added benefits programs, preferably in a Medicaid or managed care setting  Familiarity with evidence-based practices, interventions, and services for youth with behavioral health needs.  Knowledge of HIPAA regulations and confidentiality requirements.  Exceptional communication and interpersonal skills, with the ability to collaborate effectively with a diverse range of stakeholders  Excellent analytical and problem-solving skills, with the ability to gather and interpret data to inform decision-making

Education  Bachelor's degree in healthcare administration, public health, social work, or a related field.  A Master's degree is preferred
